Title: Ember Incursion

Idea: In a world where wildfires dominate vast landscapes, players manage a firefighting squad tasked with navigating through a dense forest to control and extinguish raging infernos. The forest is shrouded in a mysterious fog of war that dynamically changes with fire and wind conditions, revealing and concealing different areas in real-time. Players must strategically manage resources, plan movements, and respond to timed events and sudden wind shifts to avoid being consumed by the flames.

Features:
- Dynamic Fog of War: The fog conceals parts of the forest, only revealing sections influenced by heat, movement, or firefighting activities. Fog density alters based on wind direction, fire intensity, and players' actions.

- Squad-Based Management: Players control a team of firefighters, each with unique roles (e.g., hose operator, lookout, axe handler, drone pilot) which are necessary for effectively combating fires and navigating challenging terrains.

- Realistic Physical Sensations: The game emphasizes on immersive physical sensations such as heat waves, smoke density, and spatial audio that simulate the intense conditions of a real wildfire scenario. 

- Event System: Implements timed and cyclic events like wind gusts that shift fog and fire positions unpredictably, rain that temporarily dampens flames, or flare-ups that intensify the threat.

- Resource Management: Players must balance water supplies, tool durability, and team stamina. Refilling resources requires venturing into dangerous zones but is essential for sustained operations.

- Environmental Elements: Diverse terrains like rocky outcrops, rivers, and wind tunnels alter game tactics, providing both challenges and opportunities to leverage strategic advantages.

- Drone Reconnaissance: Utilize drones to scout through the fog and gain temporary visibility, crucial in mapping out fire spread and planning paths for avoidance and engagement.

Player Challenge:
- Strategically manage the foggy landscape by anticipating fire movements, staying clear of sudden flare-ups, and mastering real-time squad coordination.
- Efficiently juggle resource usage while ensuring the team's safety amidst unpredictable events and the physical threat of the environment.

Design Goals:
Immersive Sensation: Create an intense, deeply engaging physical sensation that accurately conveys the chaos and urgency of controlling wildfire.

Strategic Depth: Encourage strategic thinking and adaptability through innovative simulation elements, responsive fog mechanics, and a live dynamic environment.

Replayability: Introduce altering fog patterns, unpredictable natural events, and diverse terrains that guarantee unique player experiences for each gameplay session.

Environmental Awareness: Use the game's theme to raise awareness about the real-life impact of wildfires and the importance of environmental conservation efforts.

Gameplay: 
In "Ember Incursion," players take on the role of a chief firefighter, strategically commanding a squad tasked with combating the dynamic and unpredictable threat of forest fires. The objective is to manage resources effectively, strategically deploy squad members, and make tactical decisions to navigate through the forest, avoiding danger and controlling the blaze. Below is a detailed exploration of gameplay mechanics and how players would interact with the environment:

### Game Mechanics & Player Abilities

#### Squad Control:
- **Unit Selection**: Players start by selecting a well-rounded squad consisting of diverse specialists. Each member has a specific skill set that players can optimize to suit their tactical playstyle. The player can switch between squad members at will or issue commands from a top-down strategic interface.
  
- **Squad Roles**:
  - **Hose Operator**: Possesses the ability to spray water or fire suppressant foam. Ideal for dousing smaller fires and creating firebreaks.
  - **Lookout**: Equipped with binoculars for extended line-of-sight in the fog and the ability to predict wind changes slightly ahead of time.
  - **Axe Handler**: Specializes in clearing paths through dense brush, also capable of cutting down small trees to create firebreaks.
  - **Drone Pilot**: Operates reconnaissance drones, sending them into the fog to reveal hidden firefronts and plan safe pathways.

#### Environment Interaction:

- **Fog of War Dynamics**:
  - The fog system conceals and reveals parts of the forest based on environmental factors and player actions. As players move through the forest, fog retreats, but quickly reverts if left unmonitored, requiring continuous strategic navigation.
  - Players can use fire to their advantage by selectively allowing small sections to burn, revealing further areas while carefully managing these burns to prevent uncontrolled expansion.

- **Terrain Types**:
  - **Dense Forest**: Requires strategic clearing and pathfinding since these areas have higher fire risks but conceal valuable resources.
  - **Open Fields**: Offer rapid movement but make players vulnerable due to high visibility and limited cover.
  - **Rivers and Lakes**: Natural firebreaks provide temporary protection but are challenging to traverse, often requiring squad cooperation.
  
#### Tactical Systems and Player Decisions:

- **Resource Management**:
  - Players need to monitor water supply levels on their hose operators. The ability to refill at certain water sources or tanker trucks is limited, urging players to make every drop count.
  - Axe durability affects how quickly an area can be cleared or a tree felled. Players must balance equipment use before returning to camp for repairs.
  
- **Timed and Cyclic Events**:
  - Wind gusts and changes impact fire spread speed, offering opportunities for strategic maneuvers or risks of rapidly shifting situations.
  - Rain showers occasionally dampen flares temporarily, allowing players a moment of respite to regroup and plan.
  
- **Reconnaissance & Planning**:
  - The use of drones allows players to temporarily clear the fog of war over strategic areas to assess fire movements and avoid dangerous zones.
  - Deploy strategic markers using the in-game tactical map to coordinate squad members when splitting the team to achieve simultaneous objectives such as creating firefighting perimeter zones.

### Emotional and Physical Sensation
- The game is designed to immerse players through realistic audio-visual effects, such as the crackling of flames, the density of smoke, and the subtle sway of trees in wind corridors. The player’s proximity to fire influences their visual clarity, introducing elements of tension and urgency.
  
- The game’s soundtrack dynamically changes with events, guiding the player's emotional response as they face fire’s unpredictable nature, whether it’s the quiet before an impending flare-up or the frantic pace required during a defensive stand against spreading flames.

### Replayability and Strategy

- Each playthrough presents different forest layouts, distinct resource points, diverse weather patterns, and unique fire behavior, ensuring no two experiences are the same.
  
- Players earn upgrades for their squad and unlock advanced equipment by successfully completing missions, offering ways to tailor playstyles for future sessions and face more challenging scenarios.

Overall, "Ember Incursion" expertly blends tactical squad management with dynamic environmental challenges, encouraging players to anticipate and react to the evolving landscape. The intricate balance between careful resource planning, timely tactical decisions, and conquering the dense fog of war leads to a captivating and pulse-pounding simulation experience.
